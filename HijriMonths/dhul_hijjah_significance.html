• Dhul Hijjah is the twelfth month of the Islamic Hijri calendar
  • Dhul Hijjah is one of the four sacred months . The other three are Dhul Qadah, Muharram and Rajab
  • Dhul Hijjah is literally "possessor of Hajj" since this is the month of Hajj, the pilgrimage
  • Allah (swt) takes an oath by the first ten days of this month: “By the dawn; by the ten nights” (al-Fajr 89:1-2)
  • Tradition of the Prophet (ﷺ): There are no days during which righteous deeds are more beloved to Allah than these days (Sunan Ibn Majah)
  • Tradition of the Prophet (ﷺ): Fasting the Day of Arafat will be credited with Allah by forgiving one’s sins of the previous year and the following year (Muslim)
